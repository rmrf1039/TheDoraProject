5 Interesting things in Denmark

Tivoli

Crazy Friday Night Amusementpark

Tivoli is a asumement park in front of main station of Copenhagen. We went there at Friday night, when most local teenagers get drunk and stay up late to have fun there. Unfortunately, we got a curfew cause we are going to meet up our host family the next morning, so we went back to hotel at 10. I hope I can return there and experience a true night in Tivoli.

Tips: Don't forget your Copenhagen card (if you want to get DRUNK)

Little Mermaid

under the sea, under the sea

It's one of the most famous tourist spot in Denmark. The interesting part about the little mermaid is that it's actually much smaller than I thought. And if you want to have a better view on it, go there in early in the morning before sunrise. You can also enjoy the vibe of riding on a bike along the exotic street view.

Tips: 4AM is THE time to visit with sunrise along

GeoCenter Møns Klint

museum with birth of Denmark

It's a geography museum my host family took me. The museum is mainly about how Denmark was form initially. The interesting part is that it was exhibiting the first dinosuar fossil found in Denmark, so I got to see a real fossil. Also, near by is Møns Klint, a chalk cliff which happens to be the highest altitude in Denmark, only 128 m above sea level.

Tips: Don't be shy to get in a role play with the charaters, it's gonna be unforgetable

Tips: Host family is your best option for accomedation if you're on a budget

Kronborg Castle

aka Home of Hamlet

My favorite castle among all the other identical ones with different names. This is the castle where Hamlet, a classic play by Shakespere took place. There are special events and little plays occur form time to time in the castle. You might also be a part of the play if lucky enough. This is the most impressive part and change my mind of what can a history of castle be presented.

Tips:Don't be shy to get in a role play with the charaters, it's gonna be unforgetable
