National Buoyancy Lab

Zero Gravity Simulation in Water

We have visited the Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory, as known as the NBL. NBL is reponsible for training astronauts to operate in the zero gravity envirnment, such as the International Space Station. Therefore, we can see some real astronauts inside and some facilities used to train them.

Inside the lab, we can see a giant pool with an orginal size of the international space station under water. The reason why people need to learn under water is because they can experience zero gravity with a critical condition which the floating force and down force are equivalent, so the suit stay at a level.

TIPS: Remember to bring a telescope because NBL is huge.
